# Kumusta Mundo

## Ang Kaarawan ng Lokal Networks sa Pilipinas

*The Birth of the Philippine Internet*

---

Before March 29, 1994, the Philippines existed in a kind of digital isolation. Universities,
government agencies, and businesses ran their own **Bulletin Board Systems (BBS)** and **UUCP
networks** — islands of data in a pre-connected archipelago. Sending an email to someone overseas
required routing through expensive international phone calls, and even then, delivery was never
guaranteed.

Then, at **1:15 AM on March 29, 1994**, everything changed.

---

## The Historic Connection

Local tech pioneers — working under the multi-institutional **Philnet project** — successfully
linked a **Cisco 7000 router** at a PLDT facility in Makati to a **64 kbps Sprint leased line**
in the United States. In doing so, the Philippines became one of the first countries in Southeast
Asia to establish a full **TCP/IP connection** to the global Internet.

This was not just a technical achievement. It was the moment the Philippine archipelago stopped
being a collection of isolated networks and became part of the world.

## What Came After

The 1994 connection set off a chain of developments that shaped Philippine digital life:

- **1994–1996** — The first commercial Internet Service Providers (ISPs) emerge, including
  PhilNet, MOZCOM, and later PLDT's own ISP division.
- **Late 1990s** — Internet cafés spread across Metro Manila and provincial cities, democratizing
  access for students and workers who couldn't afford home connections.
- **Early 2000s** — The rise of online communities, Friendster, and early OFW communication
  platforms that kept Filipino families connected across the diaspora.
- **2010s** — Mobile internet expansion, with Smart and Globe rolling out 3G and later LTE
  networks reaching even remote barangays.
- **Today** — The Philippines is one of the world's most active social media nations, with
  internet usage deeply woven into daily life, commerce, and governance.

---

## The People Behind It

The Philnet project was a collaboration between DOST (Department of Science and Technology),
ASTI (Advanced Science and Technology Institute), PLDT, and several Philippine universities.
Key figures like **Benjie Tan** played critical roles in negotiating the international link
and configuring the infrastructure — often working through the night to make the connection happen.
